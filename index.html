About the Race

The Upper Kuttanad Boat Race is a celebration of Kerala's rich cultural heritage and the spirit of teamwork. Held annually, this event attracts thousands of spectators and features the finest snake boats (Chundan Vallams) from across the region.

History & Tradition

The Upper Kuttanad Boat Race has a history spanning over five decades. What started as a local event has now become a grand spectacle, drawing crowds from all over the world. The race is not just a competition, but a celebration of Kerala's rich maritime heritage and the spirit of unity and teamwork.

The Chundan Vallams, or snake boats, are a symbol of Kerala's craftsmanship and tradition. Each boat is meticulously crafted and can accommodate up to 100 rowers, making the race a breathtaking display of synchronization and strength.
